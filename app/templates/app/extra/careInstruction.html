Men's Clothing Care Instructions

Professional care tips to keep your wardrobe looking fresh and lasting longer

Washing

Turn inside out before washing. Use cold water (30°C) and gentle cycle. Avoid bleach to prevent color fading and fabric damage.

Drying

Air dry flat or hang on a hanger. Avoid tumble drying as heat can shrink cotton and damage fibers. Direct sunlight may cause color fading.

Ironing

Iron on medium heat while slightly damp for best results. Use steam function for stubborn creases. Iron inside out to prevent shine marks.

Storage

Hang on quality hangers to maintain shape. Store in a cool, dry place. Use cedar blocks to prevent moths and maintain freshness.

Washing

Wash inside out in cold water to preserve color. Use gentle cycle and mild detergent. Avoid over-washing - jeans can be worn 3-4 times before washing.

Drying

Air dry on a hanger or lay flat. Avoid tumble drying as it causes shrinkage and weakens denim fibers. Air drying maintains the fit and shape.

Ironing

Iron on medium to high heat. For jeans, use steam to relax fibers. Iron while slightly damp for easier crease removal. Focus on seams and pockets.

Storage

Fold neatly or hang on quality hangers. Store in a dry place to prevent mildew. Avoid overstuffing closets to maintain proper shape.

Washing

Check care label - many jackets require special care. Wash hoodies in cold water on gentle cycle. Turn jackets inside out if recommended. Use front-loading washer if possible.

Drying

Air dry hoodies flat or on a hanger. For jackets, air dry only to prevent damage to technical fabrics and insulation. Never tumble dry down jackets.

Ironing

Iron hoodies on low heat, avoiding printed areas and embroidered details. Most technical jackets should not be ironed - use steam only if specified on care label.

Storage

Hang on sturdy hangers to maintain shape. Store in a ventilated area. Use garment bags for expensive jackets. Stuff sleeves and shoulders when hanging.

Washing

Dry clean suits and blazers only. Dress shirts can be machine washed in cold water on gentle cycle. Use quality fabric softener. Avoid chlorine bleach.

Drying

Air dry dress shirts on hangers immediately after washing. Never tumble dry formal wear. Hang suits and blazers in a well-ventilated area to remove moisture.

Ironing

Iron dress shirts on medium heat with steam. Start with collar and cuffs. Use pressing cloth for wool suits. Professional pressing recommended for expensive formal wear.

Storage

Use wide, quality wooden hangers for suits. Store in breathable garment bags. Keep cedar blocks in closets. Allow 2-3 inches between garments for air circulation.

Washing

Wash in warm water (40°C) with mild detergent. Use a garment bag for delicate items. Avoid fabric softeners as they can reduce moisture-wicking properties.

Drying

Air dry when possible to extend fabric life. If using dryer, use low heat setting. Avoid over-drying which can damage elastic and cause shrinkage.

Ironing

Generally not necessary for innerwear. If needed, use very low heat and avoid direct contact with elastic bands. Most cotton underwear doesn't require ironing.

Storage

Store in a clean, dry drawer with good ventilation. Roll or fold neatly to save space. Replace worn items regularly for hygiene and comfort.

General Care Tips

Read Care Labels

Always check the care instructions on each garment before washing or cleaning.

Cold Water Washing

Using cold water saves energy and extends the life of your garments while preventing shrinkage.

Sort by Color

Separate light and dark colors to prevent color bleeding and maintain garment vibrancy.

Professional Cleaning

Invest in professional dry cleaning for expensive suits, blazers, and delicate fabrics.
